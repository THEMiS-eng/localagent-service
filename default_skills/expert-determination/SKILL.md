# Expert Determination

Technical dispute resolution by binding expert decision.

## Expert Determination Characteristics

**Key features:**
- Final and binding (very limited challenge grounds)
- Expert acts as decision-maker, not neutral evaluator
- Inquisitorial: Expert can investigate
- No formal hearing required
- Fast and cost-effective
- Ideal for technical/valuation disputes
- Common in rent review, construction valuation

## Prompt Rewrites

### expert determination clause | clause d'expertise | drafting
```
Draft expert determination clause:

CONTRACT TYPE: [description]
DISPUTES SUITABLE FOR ED: [list technical/valuation matters]

---

EXPERT DETERMINATION CLAUSE

[X]. EXPERT DETERMINATION

[X].1 Application

This Clause [X] applies to any dispute or difference between the Parties concerning:

(a) [the valuation of Variations];
(b) [the measurement of quantities];
(c) [the assessment of an extension of time];
(d) [the Final Account];
(e) [specify other technical/valuation matters];

(together "Technical Disputes").

[X].2 Reference to Expert Determination

If a Technical Dispute arises:

(a) Either Party may give notice to the other requiring the dispute to be referred to expert determination.

(b) The notice shall identify the Technical Dispute and the determination sought.

[X].3 Appointment of Expert

(a) The Expert shall be an independent [quantity surveyor / engineer / [specify]] with at least [10] years' experience in [construction disputes / relevant field].

(b) The Parties shall seek to agree the appointment of the Expert within [14] days of the notice under Clause [X].2.

(c) If the Parties fail to agree, either Party may request the President of the [RICS / ICE / other] to nominate the Expert.

[X].4 Expert's Terms of Appointment

The Expert shall:

(a) act as an expert and not as an arbitrator;
(b) determine his own procedure, provided that he:
    (i) gives each Party a reasonable opportunity to make submissions;
    (ii) acts fairly and impartially;
(c) provide his determination within [28 / 42] days of accepting appointment;
(d) give reasons for his determination [unless the Parties agree otherwise].

[X].5 Submissions

Each Party shall make submissions to the Expert within [14] days of the Expert's appointment, or such other period as the Expert directs.

[X].6 Expert's Determination

(a) The Expert's determination shall be final and binding on the Parties [save in the case of manifest error / fraud].

(b) The Expert may determine the allocation of his fees between the Parties.

(c) The Expert's determination may be enforced by either Party as a contractual obligation.

[X].7 Costs

Unless the Expert determines otherwise, each Party shall bear its own costs of the expert determination and the Parties shall bear the Expert's fees equally.

[X].8 Confidentiality

The expert determination and all submissions shall be confidential to the Parties and the Expert.

---

OPTIONAL ADDITIONS:

- [ ] "The Expert may appoint assessors to assist on technical matters."
- [ ] "The Expert may require the Parties to provide security for his fees."
- [ ] "Interest shall accrue on any sum determined at [rate]."
```

## Expert Determination vs Other ADR

| Factor | Expert Determination | Adjudication | Arbitration |
|--------|---------------------|--------------|-------------|
| Binding | Final and binding | Temporarily binding | Final and binding |
| Appeal/Challenge | Very limited | Can be reopened | Very limited |
| Typical issues | Technical/valuation | Any construction | Any |
| Procedure | Flexible/inquisitorial | Tight timeline | Formal |
| Typical duration | 4-8 weeks | 4-6 weeks | 12-24 months |
| Cost | Low | Low-medium | High |
| Legal representation | Often not needed | Common | Usually needed |

## When to Use Expert Determination

**Ideal for:**
- Quantum of variations
- Measurement disputes
- Valuation of interim applications
- Technical standards compliance
- Extension of time (technical)
- Defects assessment

**Less suitable for:**
- Complex multi-issue disputes
- Significant legal issues
- Credibility-dependent facts
- Where discovery needed

## Constraints

- Expert must stay within scope
- Must give reasons (unless waived)
- Act fairly to both parties
- Cannot delegate the determination
- Determination is binding as contract
- Very limited appeal grounds
- Conflict check essential
