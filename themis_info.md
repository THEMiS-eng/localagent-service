# Themis - Goddess of Justice and Divine Law

## Overview
Themis is a major figure in Greek mythology, representing divine law, order, and custom. She is one of the Titanesses and plays a crucial role in maintaining cosmic balance.

## Key Attributes
- **Domain**: Divine law, justice, order, custom, tradition
- **Generation**: Titaness (second generation of divine beings)
- **Parents**: Gaia (Earth) and Uranus (Sky)
- **Symbols**: Scales of justice, sword, blindfold

## Mythological Role
- Advisor to Zeus on matters of divine law
- Mother of the Fates (Moirai) and Seasons (Horae)
- Organizer of assemblies and proper conduct
- Guardian of oaths and hospitality

## Modern Legacy
- Symbol of justice in legal systems worldwide
- Inspiration for Lady Justice statues
- Represents the principle that law comes from divine/natural order
- Used in legal terminology and institutions

## Distinction from Dike
While Dike represents human justice and retribution, Themis embodies divine law and the natural order that governs both gods and mortals.
